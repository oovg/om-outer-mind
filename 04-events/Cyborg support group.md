**No Phones Bike Ride and Technology Talk**

This meetup explores our relationship to technology by beginning somewhere technology rarely allows us to start: in our bodies and in shared physical space.

Participants gather for a short group bike ride through the neighborhood or surrounding landscape. During the ride, phones remain unused. The intention is simple—move together, observe the environment, clear mental noise, and re-enter conversation from a grounded state of attention rather than from the continuous pull of screens and feeds.

After the ride, the group meets at a café, park, or community space for an open discussion about technology’s influence on cognition, community, creativity, and everyday life. Rather than debating technology in abstraction, the conversation emerges from a lived contrast: the experience of temporary disconnection followed by collective reflection.

This is not an anti-technology space. It is an experiment in _technological awareness_—creating conditions where participants can notice how attention, perception, and social presence change when digital mediation briefly recedes.

The format is intentionally simple:

- Short, no-phone group bike ride
- Regather in a shared space
- Open, facilitated conversation about technology and lived experience

The goal is to cultivate cognitive clarity, local community, and more intentional relationships with the tools that increasingly shape our lives.
