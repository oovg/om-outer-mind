# Life, underground

*P Rose*

I am told we left the above
long ago. Our ancestors fled to the earth
heal and give the ground back to grass.
In the Inverted City we cannot push down
deeper. The footing gets hot and viscous.
We build out, in radiating circles that are
filled with our creations and supportive spaces.

I am told they used to spend most their time in
boxes, above ground. That when you ventured out
it was easy to get lost in Paris or New York
Or even Denver. I find the loneliness so foreign;
they used to walk through life without
knowing their neighbor. They would eat food
from hands they never grasped, and extract
resources from people they could other.

Some wish their kids might walk the above,
one day. That we might return to make more
than concrete and glass and conflict.

I like to build here, underground. Where our
space is precious and shared, where our
bodies could be forgotten. I'd like to think
our spirit will live on long past the blocks
we built. A residing echo of the collective.
